### Story Foundation

- **Seed:** *Time is a predator, and the school clock is its mouth. Each day, it selects a victim, not for death, but for total erasure from existence and memory.*
- **World:** *Oakwood High School. A modern, beige-and-cinderblock institution that feels cleanly soul-crushing. The school is built on a forgotten cemetery for a failed mining town. The central clock in the main hallway is an antique, its brass face tarnished with verdigris, its pendulum a silent, heavy judgment. It is never wrong.*
- **Characters:**
    - **Leo:** A quiet, observant sophomore. He feels like a ghost already, an artist who notices the small, wrong details others miss. He is the POV character.
    - **Maya:** Leo's only real friend. Pragmatic, loud, and protective. She grounds him in reality, which makes what happens to her so devastating.
    - **Mr. Vance:** A history teacher, 45, with a hollowed-out look. He knows the school’s true history and has been trying to warn someone for years. He is a Cassandra figure.

- **Voice (Part 2):** The story is told in a close third-person limited to Leo's perspective. For the erasure, the voice shifts to a stark, clinical, almost omniscient tone for a few seconds, describing the erasure as an objective, cosmic process before snapping back to Leo's raw, fragmented internal monologue.
- **Craft (Technique):** **Pacing through syntax and prose rhythm.** Short, clipped sentences for tension. Long, descriptive sentences for sensory immersion. Dialogue will be used as a clock—banal at first, then sharp and truncated as 7:42 approaches. Internal monologue will reveal Leo's growing dread through specific, incongruous sensory details.

### Chapter Title: The Seventh Minute

**Act 1: The Predator's Territory (7:00 AM – 7:35 AM)**

- **7:00 AM - The First Wrong Note:**
    - **Sensory Detail:** The air in the hallway is cold and still, smelling of floor wax and a metallic, coppery undertone that Leo has never been able to place. The fluorescent lights hum a note that is just slightly flat. The clock’s tick is a heavy, wet-sounding *thock*.
    - **Character Beat (Leo):** Leo is the first student in the main hall. He notices the clock's second hand is frozen for a full second before it jerks. He chalks it up to a dying battery. He sketches the shadow the pendulum casts—it looks too long, like a scythe.
    - **Pacing:** Slow, languid. The reader feels the emptiness of the school. We are being shown the hunting grounds before the hunter arrives.

- **7:20 AM - The Haunt of the Familiar:**
    - **Sensory Detail:** The hallway fills with noise—lockers slamming like gunshots, the squeak of sneakers on linoleum, the booming voice of Coach Miller. It’s the sound of ordinariness, a mask over the ill-at-ease. Leo sees Mr. Vance standing by his classroom door, staring at the clock, his coffee cup trembling just slightly.
    - **Dialogue & Character Beat:**
        - Leo: "Morning, Mr. Vance. You okay?"
        - Vance (not looking away from the clock): "Have you ever felt a minute was heavier than others, Leo? Like it had a weight?"
        - **Pacing:** Dialogue is stilted, unnatural. Vance’s words are a riddle Leo dismisses. Maya arrives, laughing about a meme, shattering the tension. The normalcy is a welcome relief, but the unease lingers.

- **7:35 AM - The Point of No Return:**
    - **Sensory Detail:** The morning announcements crackle over the PA, the principal’s voice tinny and distant. The clock reads 7:35. Leo is at his locker, Maya is next to him, complaining about her first-period quiz.
    - **Internal Monologue:** *Seven minutes. Just seven minutes until the bell. Until the day officially starts. The clock's tick is louder now. He can feel it in his teeth. A headache is starting, a cold pressure behind his eyes. Why did he draw that shadow so long? It’s just a shadow.*
    - **Character Beat:** Leo locks eyes with Mr. Vance across the hall. Vance gives a single, sharp shake of his head. A warning. Leo’s stomach drops. The noise of the hallway seems to drain away, leaving only the *thock* of the pendulum.

**Act 2: The Mouth Opens (7:35 AM – 7:42 AM)**

- **7:35 - 7:41 AM - The Slow Unraveling:**
    - **Pacing:** Tense and accelerating. Sentences become shorter, more frantic.
    - **Action:** Leo turns to ask Maya if she feels it, the cold, the wrongness. She’s still talking, but her voice is a mumble. He notices the floor around Maya, a six-foot radius, is suddenly empty. Students are walking in an arc around her, unconsciously. They don't see her.
    - **Dialogue:** "Maya?" Leo whispers. She looks at him, smile fading. "Leo? You look weird." Her voice is faint, as if from a great distance.
    - **Sensory Detail:** The air around her shimmers, like heat haze on a summer road. The smell of copper is overpowering. The clock’s second hand begins to vibrate.

- **7:42:00 AM - The Erasure, Step-by-Step:**
    - **Step 1 - The Stutter:** Maya's eyes go wide. She stops mid-sentence. She looks down at her hands as if she has never seen them.
    - **Step 2 - The Shimmer (Voice shifts to clinical, omniscient):** *For a moment, the space occupied by the girl exists in a slipstream. A static charge builds in the air, making all hair stand on end. The light bends around her form, not hitting her, but passing through. She is becoming a negative space.*
    - **Step 3 - The Unbecoming:** Her color drains, not to pale, but to a perfect, featureless gray, like a statue. The details of her face begin to soften and dissolve—the curve of her smile, the kindness in her eyes, the specific shape of her lips—all erasing like a chalk drawing under a wet rag.
    - **Step 4 - The Sound (Leo's perspective returns):** A low, sub-audible hum that turns into a sound like a radio between stations, full of static and forgotten voices. Leo sees her mouth open in a silent scream that grows thinner, higher, until it is the whine of a mosquito, then nothing.
    - **Step 5 - The Absence:** At 7:42:45, the shimmer collapses inward with a soft, wet *pop*. Where Maya stood, there is nothing. No girl. No backpack. Not even a single hair on the linoleum floor. The air is still. The copper smell vanishes. The clock's second hand resumes its measured, heavy sweep.
    - **Sensory Detail:** The hallway is silent. The clock is the only sound. *Thock. Thock. Thock.*

**Act 3: The Echo of the Hollow (7:43 AM – End of Chapter)**

- **7:43 AM - The Gaslight:**
    - **Sensory Detail:** The silence breaks. The chatter of students returns, but it feels hollow, like a recording. Leo is standing alone. He looks at the spot where Maya was. A single M&M from her snack pack lies on the floor, crushed.
    - **Dialogue:**
        - A passing girl: "Dude, you okay? You look like you saw a ghost."
        - Leo, hoarse: "Where's Maya?"
        - The girl, confused: "Who's Maya? The new girl? Your lab partner?"
    - **Character Beat (Leo):** Panic. He asks another student. They don't know her. He asks a teacher. The teacher says he doesn't have a partner named Maya in his class. Her name is a sound that no one recognizes. Her existence is a fiction.

- **7:50 AM - The Revelation:**
    - **Internal Monologue (Leo):** *No. No, no, no. I remember her laugh. The way she snorted. I drew her portrait last week. It’s in my sketchbook. I’ll prove it.* He frantically opens his locker.
    - **Sensory Detail:** The sketchbook is where he left it. He opens it to the portrait of Maya. The paper is blank. A perfect, unmarred white rectangle, as if he never drew anything.
    - **Character Beat (Mr. Vance):** Vance appears beside him, silent. He looks old, defeated. "It's happening again," he whispers, "It chose one of yours this time." He points a trembling finger at the clock. "It always takes the one who is most loved. It's hungry."

- **7:55 AM - The Chilling Hint (End of Chapter):**
    - **Action:** Leo, stunned, looks at Mr. Vance's finger. Then at the clock.
    - **The Hint:** The clock’s minute hand, currently on the 7 for 7:55, is moving. But it’s not moving forward. The second hand is slowing down. Stuttering.
    - **Final Sensory Detail:** The air feels cold again. The faint scent of copper returns. The clock’s pendulum, heavy and still for a moment, gives one, single, premonitory *swing*.
    - **Final Internal Monologue:** *It’s hungry. It’s not just a clock that erases at 7:42. That was just today’s feeding. How long until the next? How many more students will it take before the school is just a building full of empty desks, with no one to remember them? And worst of all… he looks down at his own hand, which is pale against his dark jeans.* *Is it just the cold? Or am I starting to look… translucent?*
