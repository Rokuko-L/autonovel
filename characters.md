### 1. Protagonist – Mia Torres (16, Student)
- **Age / Role:** 16 years old, junior at Westbrook High School. She’s a quiet art student who keeps a sketchbook of the school’s hallways.
- **Personality:** Observant, introspective, and prone to anxiety. Mia notices small details others miss – like the way the clock’s second hand stutters just before the hour. She trusts her gut, even when it makes her look paranoid. After witnessing the first erasure (a classmate vanishing mid-laugh at 7:42), she becomes fixated on proving it’s real.
- **Relation to the Clock:** **Target & Witness** – She is the first to directly see the erasure happen (she blinked and the seat was empty – no one else remembers the student). The clock “sees” her noticing, and soon she feels it watching her during each morning countdown. She begins drawing the erased people from vague memories no one else has, trying to keep them alive.
- **Voice Nuance:** Soft, hesitant, often trailing off. Uses tentative phrases like *“I think… maybe… it’s not just my imagination?”* Her internal monologue is full of sensory details (the taste of stale air, the hum of the fluorescent lights) – she experiences the horror viscerally.

### 2. Foil – Derek Novak (17, Student & Rival)
- **Age / Role:** 17 years old, senior class president. Star debate team captain, future valedictorian. He’s Mia’s intellectual rival – they clashed in a school board meeting over art funding.
- **Personality:** Arrogant, pragmatic, and loudly skeptical. Derek believes in logic, evidence, and the power of the scientific method. He mocks Mia’s “delusional ghost stories” and actively tries to disprove her by filming the clock at 7:42 for a week straight. He’s charismatic enough to turn other students against her.
- **Relation to the Clock:** **Denial & Unwitting Target** – He refuses to accept any supernatural explanation, insisting the missing students transferred out or dropped out. But the clock has started to “tag” him – his reflection in the clock’s glass face appears a second delayed. He secretly feels a prickling sensation at 7:41 each morning, but he rationalizes it as a caffeine jitter. His denial will be his undoing.
- **Voice Nuance:** Sharp, fast-talking, condescending. Uses rhetorical questions: *“You honestly think a clock can erase people? Come on, Mia – Occam’s razor.”* When he’s alone, his muttering becomes fragmented: *“It’s just… just a glitch. Yes. Glitch.”*

### 3. Secret Knowledge – Mr. Alistair Thorne (58, Janitor)
- **Age / Role:** 58 years old, head custodian at Westbrook High. He’s been here since the school opened 35 years ago. He was an art teacher until a “nervous breakdown.”
- **Personality:** Stoic, watchful, and melancholy. He speaks rarely and in clipped warnings. His left hand is missing two fingers – a souvenir from a 7:42 incident 20 years ago when he tried to smash the clock. He keeps a hidden folder in the boiler room with photos of every erased student, starting from 1989.
- **Relation to the Clock:** **Obsession & Guardian** – He knows the clock is a sentient trap built by the school’s founder (a clockmaker who lost his daughter at 7:42 AM). Thorne’s mission is to prevent anyone from being erased again. He has tried everything: unplugging it, covering it, even breaking its gears – but it always repairs itself. He now spends every morning at 7:41 standing in front of the clock, hoping to be taken instead of the children.
- **Voice Nuance:** Low, gravelly, with long pauses. He says things like: *“Don’t look at it. Don’t think about it. The clock knows your name. It hears your heartbeat.”* He never answers questions directly – only offers cryptic truths.

### 4. Secondary Secret / Antagonist – Principal Lorraine Vance (52, School Principal)
- **Age / Role:** 52 years old, principal for 12 years. She is polished, well-liked by parents, and obsessed with school statistics – including attendance and graduation rates.
- **Personality:** Manipulative, high-functioning, and deeply afraid. She knows about the clock – she inherited a memo from the previous principal (“The 7:42 Protocol”). She has been covering up erasures by altering records, claiming students transferred, and gaslighting witnesses with the help of a complicit guidance counselor. She believes (or pretends) that ignoring the clock makes it weaker.
- **Relation to the Clock:** **Obsession & Control** – She is not a target (the clock seems to spare authority figures who don’t directly challenge it). Instead, she is a facilitator. She has tried to “feed” the clock students who were academic liabilities – troublemakers, low scorers – so that the “strong” students survive. She rationalizes this as a cruel necessity to keep the school running. She keeps a private journal detailing each erasure’s time and the victim’s last expression.
- **Voice Nuance:** Polished, warm, but with a steel edge. She always says *“We must move forward. For the students.”* But when alone, her calm breaks: *“It’s hungry again… who can I spare tomorrow?”*
