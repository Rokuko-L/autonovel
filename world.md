### Setting Document: Seraphim Academy

**1. The School & Its Atmosphere**

- **Name:** Seraphim Academy for the Gifted.
- **Location:** Urban. A single, gloomy neo-Gothic block tucked into a forgotten corner of a decaying industrial city. The surrounding streets are a maze of empty lots and boarded-up factories. The city itself feels like a hollowed-out corpse.
- **Architecture:** Old Victorian, but perverted. The original 1880s structure has been “modernized” over the decades in the cheapest way possible: asbestos siding over gargoyles, fluorescent lights buzzing in walnut-paneled libraries. The windows are tall and arched, but many are painted shut or bricked over. The main building is a central, brooding tower flanked by two lower wings. A new, sterile gymnasium clings to one side like a plastic tumor.
- **Atmosphere:** Oppressive. It is a silence of held breath. The air is thick with the smell of floor wax, mildew, and faint, sweet rot. The heating system clanks and groans, a mechanical heart that never rests. Shadows seem to move in the periphery, but never when you look directly. There is a constant, low-frequency hum that may just be the lights. The only thing that is truly, vibrantly alive is the clock.

**2. The Culture & Daily Life**

The school's "gifted" program is a lie. The students are not the best and brightest, but the most disposable—troubled kids from broken homes, quiet orphans, the ones no one would miss. They are taught to be quiet, to obey, and most importantly, to never be late.

Daily life is a ritual of dread.
- **Morning Bell:** 7:30 AM. Students file in under the dead gaze of the Headmistress, a woman whose face is a permanent, tight smile. No one speaks in the halls.
- **The Countdown:** From 7:30 to 7:42, a palpable panic spreads. Teachers begin their lessons, but no one learns. All eyes flicker, again and again, to the classroom clock.
- **The Event:** 7:42 AM. The school holds its breath. The clock’s second hand, which has been jerking silently for twelve minutes, stops. The chime sounds—a single, long, low 'BONG' that sounds like it comes from the bottom of a well. Then, a soft, papery rustle. A desk is empty. A bag slumps.
- **Aftermath:** The remaining students wipe the dust from their shoulders. The teacher erases the missing student’s name from the seating chart without a word. The lesson continues. There is no memorial, no police report. The name is never spoken again. The new student who "transfers in" a week later will find an unassigned locker and a dusty desk.
- **The Unspoken Rule:** The only crime at Seraphim is to be noticed by the clock.

**3. The Lore of the Clock**

- **Origin:** The clock is not the original. It was installed in 1924 by a student named Elias Thorne, a prodigy in mechanics and thaumaturgy. He was obsessed with trapping a perfect moment, a "point of pure potential." His experiment in temporal phasing went wrong. He didn't trap a moment; he created a predator that feeds on them. The clock is the cage, and the students are the meat. Elias’s name is not on any plaque. His final journal entry simply reads: *“The teeth are set. 7:42 is the hour of the bite.”*

- **Previous Victims:** The records are blank. The school’s official, incinerated archives are a myth. But the old custodian, a man with skin like cracked leather and no tongue, has a ritual. Every evening, he dusts a row of 8,000 small, identical brass nameplates in a sealed sub-basement. The plates are for students who have walked these halls since 1924. He dusts a new one every day. He never has to guess which name to add.

- **Known Rules:**
    - **The Call:** At 7:42, the clock’s chime is irresistible to its chosen victim. It is not a compulsion of the mind, but of the body. A student might try to run, but their feet will take them to their chair. They might scream, but their mouth will close. They simply become a passenger in their own body for that single, final second.
    - **Avoidance is Futile:** The clock is not looking for a specific person. It is looking for a *state*. It isn't attracted to the late student or the early one. It is attracted to the student whose life has the least *tension* at that precise moment. The one who, for a split second, has no plans, no goals, no future. The daydreamer. The hopeless one. You cannot hide. You can only try very, very hard to be interesting.
    - **The Permanence:** To be erased is to be *un-existed*. Birth certificates fade. Photographs turn to blank paper. Parents weep for a child they can’t name. The world doesn't remember the hole you were taken from. The clock fills it with a new, forgettable life.

**Ominous, Detached Caution:** The hallways are very long. The lockers are very cold. The clocks in every room are perfectly synchronized. It is best to keep your head down, your mind loud with problems, and your heart full of desperate, frantic plans. It is best to be interesting. The last person who stopped being interesting was here yesterday. You don’t remember them. Do you?
